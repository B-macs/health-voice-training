# Voice-training activity catalogue and safety envelope

Updated: 2026-07-14

## What changed

The original Days 1-10 baseline is unchanged. The Training tab now has a
separate selectable **Training Library** containing all 22 Activity cards:
the 12 original baseline activities and the ten additional activities below.
Every item uses the existing Activity card template: title, duration,
category, countdown seconds, and four illustrated explanation steps. No
voice-analysis, Voice Quality scoring, recording protocol, or database
behaviour changed.

Starting an activity from the library is optional standalone practice. It does
not mark a daily-plan item complete, change XP, streak, history, or plan
progress, and it never auto-starts the next plan card. The library remains
available while the next plan day is locked and after the 10-day baseline is
complete. `EXERCISE_LIBRARY` is the single source of truth: a later plan can
mix and match its stable activity ids without creating a second catalogue.
`NEW_RECORDING` stays a daily-plan capture step rather than a library item.

### Supplied connected-speech paragraph

Seven cards now display one stable, three-sentence paragraph exactly where
connected speech is requested: Pulmo-Train Reading Carryover, Twang
Brightness, Resonant Phrase Carryover, Gentle Phrase Pacing, Easy
Articulation Practice, Chant-to-Speech Bridge, and Cool-Down & Carryover
Check. Use one sentence per easy exhale and pause naturally at full stops;
the twang and chant cards intentionally use the first sentence only.

The paragraph follows `ANALYSIS_LANGUAGE` (currently German) so practice has
consistent language, but it is deliberately separate from the short,
versioned recording-capture passage. It does not change recording protocol,
Voice Quality scoring, provenance, or historical comparability. Brief Voice
Recovery Break remains quiet-rest-first: its single optional closing sentence
is not expanded into a paragraph.

## Why these activities are deliberately gentle

Health's patient profile records Stage 1 rehabilitation, an active
mid-/lower-back flare, and generalised hypermobility. These voice cards do
not prescribe a posture correction, a prolonged held position, bending,
twisting, bracing, or physical loading. Each can be done in a supported chair
with feet supported or an easy neutral standing position, and the user is
asked to change position rather than push through back symptoms.

The latest Voice Training recording was accepted for analysis but marked
quality-limited because the sustained-vowel signal-to-noise estimate was low.
That makes it unsuitable for score-driven progression or a conclusion about
voice change. The new activities therefore focus on low-effort practice and
repeatable capture conditions, not on chasing a Voice Quality score. For
future comparable captures, use a quiet room and keep microphone, distance,
and reading task consistent.

## Activity intent

| Activity | Intent and distinction from the original pool |
| --- | --- |
| Supported Voice Reset | Establishes a comfortable, non-held setup and a light mid-range hum. It is not a body-rehabilitation drill. |
| Lip Trill Ease | A device-free semi-occluded vocal-tract option, distinct from the Pulmo-Train tube work. |
| Voiced /v/ Flow | Uses a gentle voiced-fricative-to-vowel transition rather than an s/z duration comparison. |
| Nasal Resonance Ladder | Transfers light m/n/ng resonance into a vowel, rather than the original mmm-to-ah sequence. |
| Resonant Phrase Carryover | Carries low-effort resonance into everyday speech without the Pulmo-Train. |
| Small-Step Pitch Pattern | Uses small separated middle-range steps rather than continuous full-range glides. |
| Gentle Phrase Pacing | Practices speech phrasing and quiet breath resets rather than a maximum-duration task. |
| Easy Articulation Practice | Prioritises clarity from lips and tongue rather than loudness or projection. |
| Chant-to-Speech Bridge | Transfers an easy single-note phrase into natural speech. |
| Brief Voice Recovery Break | Makes quiet recovery and symptom checking an explicit, low-load card. |

## Safety and clinical limits

These are self-guided practice prompts, not a treatment prescription or a
replacement for ENT or speech-language-pathologist guidance. Use only a
comfortable pitch and ordinary speaking volume. Do not whisper, force
loudness or range, push through throat effort, or treat the Voice Quality
score as a diagnosis.

Stop or skip an activity for throat pain, increasing strain or hoarseness,
breathlessness, dizziness/lightheadedness, or worsening back symptoms.
Seek prompt medical advice for difficulty breathing, pain when speaking or
swallowing, coughing blood, sudden loss of voice, or persistent hoarseness;
the NIDCD advises clinical evaluation for these warning signs and for
hoarseness lasting more than three weeks.

## Evidence boundaries

Lip trills, straw/tube phonation, water resistance, voiced /v/, and nasal
/m/ and /n/ are all semi-occluded vocal-tract configurations described in
voice research. Their effects vary by exercise and person, so the cards use
short, non-escalating practice and retain SLP review as the deciding
authority rather than assuming that one technique will work for everyone.

- [Robieux et al., direct measurements of semi-occluded vocal-tract exercises](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24850270/)
- [Dargin et al., variable laryngeal response across semi-occluded exercises](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26390961/)
- [NIDCD: Hoarseness](https://www.nidcd.nih.gov/health/hoarseness)
